## 1. The divisors A and B `[PROVEN combinatorics — h2_divisors.py]`

octagon): `1↦0, 2↦t₁, 3↦t₂, 4↦t₃, 5↦t₄, 6↦t₅, 7↦1, 8↦∞`, so a factor `tᵢ−tⱼ`

**A = polar divisor of ω** (the `dt`-line denominator
`(t₃−t₁)·t₃·(1−t₄)·(t₄−t₂)·(t₅−t₂)`):

| factor | collision | boundary divisor |
|---|---|---|
| (t₃−t₁) | z₄,z₂ | δ₂₄ |
| t₃ | z₄,z₁ | δ₁₄ |
| (1−t₄) | z₇,z₅ | δ₅₇ |
| (t₄−t₂) | z₅,z₃ | δ₃₅ |
| (t₅−t₂) | z₆,z₃ | δ₃₆ |

**A = {δ₂₄, δ₁₄, δ₅₇, δ₃₅, δ₃₆}** (5 divisors).
**Cross-check `[PROVEN]`:** these are exactly the subscripts of the exponents
`b₂₄, b₁₄, b₅₇, b₃₅, b₃₆` in eq. (I1) — the script confirms
`A == {δ_ij : ij ∈ b-subscripts}` is `True`. Strong internal consistency: the
exponent labels of BZ *are* the polar boundary divisors.

**B = Zariski closure of the domain boundary** = the 20 facets of the M̄₀,₈
associahedron = the consecutive-interval divisors δ_S (S an interval in the cyclic

t₁=0,…,t₅=1 are the length-2 intervals δ₁₂,δ₂₃,δ₃₄,δ₄₅,δ₅₆,δ₆₇; the other 14 arise
in the compactification (associahedron corners), including δ₇₈, δ₈₁ "at infinity".

**A ∩ B = ∅** `[PROVEN]`: no polar divisor is a domain facet — the integrand is
regular on the boundary of the simplex, as it must be for a convergent cellular
integral. `M̄₀,₈` has 119 boundary divisors total = (2⁸−2−16)/2 `[PROVEN]`.

an M₀,₈-embedding effect on the specific gr^W₄ torus, not a feature of the abstract
ζ(2)-object.** This localizes the whole question to the 2-divisibility of
`proj(T)` in the integral rank-1 gr^W₄ of `H⁵(M̄₀,₈∖A, B; ℤ)`.

**5b. The order-2 involution i₁ — two clean results.** `[COMPUTED — h2_symmetry.py]`
i₁ fixes the symmetric point (`i₁(1,…,1) = (1,…,1)`) and is motivic.

* **(refuted mechanism)** i₁ is **not** a marked-point permutation: among the 16
  octagon dihedral symmetries of the domain, **only the identity preserves A**.
  So the p=2 factor is *not* a cheap S₈-orbit artifact.
* **(visible action)** In the cubical chart (I-a), i₁ = (x₁x₅)(x₂x₄) pairs the four
  polar factors `1−xₖxₖ₊₁` into two 2-cycles (P₁↔P₄, P₂↔P₃) and acts on the

  the fixed stratum {P₁,P₄} on which i₁ swaps the two dlog's (eigenvalue −1).

**5c. Why i₁ does not by itself settle the index.** `[COMPUTED]` ω is i₁-invariant
with ω₂-coefficient c₂ = −4P̂ₙ ≠ 0 for n≥1 (c₂ = −101 at n=1). Functoriality then
**forces i₁ = +1 on gr^W₄** (both realizations). Consequences:
* the fixed stratum {P₁,P₄} (eigenvalue −1) contributes to the *anti*-invariant
  part, so it is **not** seen by the invariant ω₂ — the invariant gr^W₄ is carried

But i₁ = +1 means averaging `(T + i₁T)/2` gives no new integral class, and i₁ does
**not** decide whether `proj(T₁₃)` is primitive (index 1) or 2×primitive (index 2).
The order-2 symmetry pins the *prime* (only 2 can appear — no order-3 element
exists, consistent with index 1 at p=3) but not the *value* at p=2.

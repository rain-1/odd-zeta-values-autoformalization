# H2: the integral Betti lattice of the Brown–Zudilin rank-3 motive at the symmetric point

**Task.** Compute the integral Betti lattice data of the rank-3 motive `M`
(gr^W = ℚ(0) ⊕ ℚ(−2) ⊕ ℚ(−5)) of the totally symmetric ₈π₈ cellular integral,
and determine the lattice index that the measured denominator constants predict —
confirming or refuting the H2 hypothesis of `PROOF_MECHANISM.md`.

**Prediction to test (from `CONJECTURE.md` / §3 of `PROOF_MECHANISM.md`):** the
integral Betti lattice refines the naive (γ₁, γ₂)-span by **index 2 at p=2** and
**index 1 at p=3** (P-side), so that the abstract elimination cost 24 = 2³·3 of
Lemma 1 drops to the measured sharp constant 12 = 2²·3.

**Scope honesty.** This is the "one finite computation, never done" (H2). A fully
rigorous integral relative-homology computation of `H⁵(M̄₀,₈∖A, B; ℤ)` is a
paper-scale undertaking; per the task's rule (iv) I reduce to the decisive rank-2
sub-piece (the ζ(2)-elimination lattice), do everything that is checkable
rigorously, and flag the single residual finite question. Every step is tagged
`[PROVEN]` / `[COMPUTED]` / `[CONVENTION-RISK]`.

Scripts: `h2_dictionary.py`, `h2_divisors.py`, `h2_periods.py`, `h2_symmetry.py`

## 1. The divisors A and B `[PROVEN combinatorics — h2_divisors.py]`

Marked-point chart (points on the real line in cyclic order 1..8 around the
octagon): `1↦0, 2↦t₁, 3↦t₂, 4↦t₃, 5↦t₄, 6↦t₅, 7↦1, 8↦∞`, so a factor `tᵢ−tⱼ`
is the collision divisor δ of the two carrying points.

**A = polar divisor of ω** (the `dt`-line denominator
`(t₃−t₁)·t₃·(1−t₄)·(t₄−t₂)·(t₅−t₂)`):

| factor | collision | boundary divisor |
|---|---|---|
| (t₃−t₁) | z₄,z₂ | δ₂₄ |
| t₃ | z₄,z₁ | δ₁₄ |
| (1−t₄) | z₇,z₅ | δ₅₇ |
| (t₄−t₂) | z₅,z₃ | δ₃₅ |
| (t₅−t₂) | z₆,z₃ | δ₃₆ |

**A = {δ₂₄, δ₁₄, δ₅₇, δ₃₅, δ₃₆}** (5 divisors).
**Cross-check `[PROVEN]`:** these are exactly the subscripts of the exponents
`b₂₄, b₁₄, b₅₇, b₃₅, b₃₆` in eq. (I1) — the script confirms
`A == {δ_ij : ij ∈ b-subscripts}` is `True`. Strong internal consistency: the
exponent labels of BZ *are* the polar boundary divisors.

**B = Zariski closure of the domain boundary** = the 20 facets of the M̄₀,₈
associahedron = the consecutive-interval divisors δ_S (S an interval in the cyclic
order, 2 ≤ |S| ≤ 6). Count `n(n−3)/2 = 20` `[PROVEN]`. The 6 affine facets
t₁=0,…,t₅=1 are the length-2 intervals δ₁₂,δ₂₃,δ₃₄,δ₄₅,δ₅₆,δ₆₇; the other 14 arise
in the compactification (associahedron corners), including δ₇₈, δ₈₁ "at infinity".

**A ∩ B = ∅** `[PROVEN]`: no polar divisor is a domain facet — the integrand is
regular on the boundary of the simplex, as it must be for a convergent cellular
integral. `M̄₀,₈` has 119 boundary divisors total = (2⁸−2−16)/2 `[PROVEN]`.

## 2. ℚ-level ranks `[COMPUTED — closed formulas, self-consistent]`

Keel: `H*(M̄₀,ₙ, ℤ)` is torsion-free, generated by boundary divisors; all
cohomology is even (Tate). This torsion-freeness is exactly what promotes the
ℚ-computation to ℤ. Betti data (verified palindromic by Poincaré duality):

| n | b₂ = 2ⁿ⁻¹−C(n,2)−1 | Poincaré (even Betti) |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | 5 | 1,5,1 |
| 6 | 16 | 1,16,16,1 |
| 7 | 42 | 1,42,127,42,1 |
| **8** | **99** | **1,99,626,626,99,1** |

The **rank-3** structure of the subquotient `M` with gr^W = ℚ(0)⊕ℚ(−2)⊕ℚ(−5)
(and gr^W₈ = 0 in the symmetric case) is proven in Brown [Br16] and is confirmed
**experimentally** by the audit pipeline: every I(a·n) is recovered by PSLQ over
exactly the 3 periods {1, ζ(2), ζ₅} — no independent ζ(3) or ζ(4) coefficient
appears. That is the ℚ-level fingerprint of rank 3. `admcycles` was installed
(v1.4) but its tautological-ring API operates on the *absolute* cohomology of
M̄₀,₈, not the relative motive of the (∖A, B)-pair, so it does not directly give
`M`; the closed formulas above are the reliable cross-check.

The rank-1 gr^W₄ = ℚ(−2) piece is carried by the **codim-2 strata inside A**
= transverse crossings of pairs of polar divisors that *meet* (Keel: two δ meet
iff the subsets are nested/disjoint/complementary). `h2_divisors.py` finds **7**
such strata:
`{24,57},{24,35},{24,36},{14,57},{14,35},{14,36},{57,36}`
(the three crossing pairs 24|14, 57|35, 35|36 do **not** meet). Each transverse
crossing gives a clean (𝔾ₘ)² local model → a primitive (2πi)²-torus. The rank-1
projection cuts these 7 tori to a single generator γ₂; **its 2-divisibility is the
H2 index.**

## 5. The index computation, honestly `[PARTIAL — the crux]`

**5a. The abstract ζ(2)-motive has index 1.** `[COMPUTED]` Since
Ext¹_{MT(ℤ)}(ℚ(0),ℚ(2)) = K₃(ℤ)⊗ℚ = 0, the ℚ(0)⊕ℚ(−2) sub is a *split* extension;
its clean model (a 𝔾ₘ² torus vs dlog∧dlog) has the (2πi)²-torus **primitive** and
pairing exactly (2πi)² — index 1, cost exactly 24. **Any refinement is therefore
an M₀,₈-embedding effect on the specific gr^W₄ torus, not a feature of the abstract
ζ(2)-object.** This localizes the whole question to the 2-divisibility of
`proj(T)` in the integral rank-1 gr^W₄ of `H⁵(M̄₀,₈∖A, B; ℤ)`.

**5b. The order-2 involution i₁ — two clean results.** `[COMPUTED — h2_symmetry.py]`
i₁ fixes the symmetric point (`i₁(1,…,1) = (1,…,1)`) and is motivic.

* **(refuted mechanism)** i₁ is **not** a marked-point permutation: among the 16
  octagon dihedral symmetries of the domain, **only the identity preserves A**.
  So the p=2 factor is *not* a cheap S₈-orbit artifact.
* **(visible action)** In the cubical chart (I-a), i₁ = (x₁x₅)(x₂x₄) pairs the four
  polar factors `1−xₖxₖ₊₁` into two 2-cycles (P₁↔P₄, P₂↔P₃) and acts on the
  codim-2 strata with orbit sizes **[2,1]**: the size-2 orbit {P₁,P₃}↔{P₂,P₄}, and
  the fixed stratum {P₁,P₄} on which i₁ swaps the two dlog's (eigenvalue −1).

**5c. Why i₁ does not by itself settle the index.** `[COMPUTED]` ω is i₁-invariant
with ω₂-coefficient c₂ = −4P̂ₙ ≠ 0 for n≥1 (c₂ = −101 at n=1). Functoriality then
**forces i₁ = +1 on gr^W₄** (both realizations). Consequences:
* the fixed stratum {P₁,P₄} (eigenvalue −1) contributes to the *anti*-invariant
  part, so it is **not** seen by the invariant ω₂ — the invariant gr^W₄ is carried
  by the size-2 orbit;
* T₁₃ and T₂₄ = i₁T₁₃ have **equal image** in the rank-1 gr^W₄.

But i₁ = +1 means averaging `(T + i₁T)/2` gives no new integral class, and i₁ does
**not** decide whether `proj(T₁₃)` is primitive (index 1) or 2×primitive (index 2).
The order-2 symmetry pins the *prime* (only 2 can appear — no order-3 element
exists, consistent with index 1 at p=3) but not the *value* at p=2.

**5d. The residual finite question.** `[NOT COMPLETED — precisely stated]`
> Is the image of the primitive transverse torus `T₁₃` a primitive generator of the
> integral rank-1 `gr^W₄ H⁵(M̄₀,₈∖A, B; ℤ)`, or twice a generator?
> Equivalently: in the integral weight/boundary spectral sequence for the pair, is
> the map (2-torus of {P₁,P₃}) → gr^W₄ divisible by 2?

Index 2 ⟺ that map is 2-divisible (a global gluing relation, not a local one).
This is the one computation that would discharge H2; it needs the full integral
relative-homology bookkeeping (Gysin residues along A, relative sequence for B,
Keel-integral throughout), which I did not carry out.

## 6. Verdict

**Measurement and lattice analysis are CONSISTENT, not contradictory — but H2 is
reduced, not discharged.** Specifically:

1. `[established]` **p=3 and p≥5: index 1**, matching measurement. There is no
   mechanism for a refinement (no order-3 symmetry; M̄₀,₈ is torsion-free; the
   strata are transverse). The 3 in the cost 12 = 2²·3 is the unrefined
   von Staudt–Clausen 3 of B₂. This part of H2 is **discharged**.

2. `[reduced to one finite computation]` **p=2:** the measurement fixes the
   *product* (Betti refinement)·(de Rham normalization) = 2 at p=2 — H2 (Betti)
   and H1 (de Rham) can only be disentangled together. The abstract object is
   index 1 (§5a); the factor 2 is an M₀,₈-embedding effect localized to the
   2-divisibility of the gr^W₄ torus (§5d); the order-2 symmetry explains why the
   available prime is exactly 2 and nothing at 3 (§5b–c). Everything computable is
   **consistent with the predicted index 2 at p=2**, but I did not prove the value
   from geometry, and I explicitly **could not confirm it via the two natural cheap
   mechanisms** (S₈ orbit — refuted; i₁ averaging — neutral because i₁ = +1 on
   gr^W₄).

3. `[diagnosis, both hypotheses reported]` The residual 2 is one of:
   * **H2a (Betti refinement):** `proj(T₁₃) = 2·primitive`, i.e. γ₂/2 ∈ L. This is
     the `PROOF_MECHANISM` reading; needs a global 2-divisibility in gr^W₄.
   * **H2b (de Rham normalization):** the Betti torus is primitive (index 1) and
     the factor 2 is a residue normalization of the integral de Rham ω₂ — i.e. it
     belongs to **H1**, not H2, and H2's index is 1. The clean-𝔾ₘ² local pairing
     ⟨T₁₃, ω₂⟩ = (2πi)² and i₁ = +1 on gr^W₄ mildly favour this reading.
   I cannot separate these without computing *both* integral lattices; the
   measurement alone (12 = 24/2) fixes only their product.

**Bottom line for Theorem 2.** H2 is **not** unconditional-at-a-point yet. What is
now rigorous: the divisor setup (§1), the ℚ-ranks (§2), the "24" identity and the
measurement→index dictionary (§3–4, prediction = index 2 at p=2 / 1 at p=3), the
discharge of p=3 and p≥5 (index 1, no mechanism), and the reduction of the entire
remaining content to **a single, explicitly stated finite 2-divisibility question**
(§5d) about whether the primitive weight-4 boundary-torus is 2-divisible in the
integral gr^W₄ of the M̄₀,₈ pair — with the honest caveat that the factor may in
fact live on the H1 (de Rham) side. No setup error was found; no phenomenon beyond
the already-measured index was discovered.

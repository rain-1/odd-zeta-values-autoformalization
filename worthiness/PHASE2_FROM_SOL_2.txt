1. RIGOROUS EXACT ALGEBRA
-------------------------

Use the established normalized recurrence

  c0(m)Y_m+c1(m)Y_{m+1}+c2(m)Y_{m+2}+c3(m)Y_{m+3}=0,
  c3(m)=2(m+3)^5(2m+5)a0(m),
  a0(m)=41218m^3+198849m^2+320790m+173057.

Everything in this section was checked as an exact polynomial identity by
worthiness/sol_local_regular.py; the displayed divisions have zero remainder
over Z[m].

1.1 Midpoint row (small uniform identity).

There exist H_i(m) in Z[m] such that, coefficientwise for i=0,1,2,

  128 c_i(m) = 7 R_i + (2m+5) H_i(m),

where

  (R_0,R_1,R_2)=(11907,-334374,-19292).

Thus, if p is not 2 or 7, t=v_p(2m+5)>0,
u=v_p(a0(m))=0, and e=min_{0<=i<=2}v_p(P_{m+i}), the full midpoint regularity
condition is equivalent to the per-(m,p) certificate

  v_p(11907 P_m-334374 P_{m+1}-19292 P_{m+2}) >= e+t.       (MID)

This is valid at arbitrary prime-power depth t, not merely modulo p. Prime 7
requires a shifted certificate because the constant row itself has the extra
factor 7.

1.2 Cubic row (small uniform identity).

Put

  D = 688444586089376 = 2^5*37^5*557^2,

  A_0(m)=7502522434517190247m^2
         +27377435999133628028m+23682487355887746399,

  A_1(m)=14(3763413087280622569m^2
         +11453615933815386820m+8744992879372731313),

  A_2(m)=17052(1395403006376645m^2
         +4537329573624116m+3700105031529293).

There exist K_i(m) in Z[m] such that

  D c_i(m) = A_i(m) + a0(m) K_i(m)       (i=0,1,2).

Hence, whenever p does not divide D, u=v_p(a0(m))>0,
t=v_p(2m+5)=0, and e is as above, cubic regularity is equivalent to

  v_p(A_0(m)P_m+A_1(m)P_{m+1}+A_2(m)P_{m+2}) >= e+u.       (CUB)

Again this is a full prime-power certificate, not only a first residue digit.
Prime 37 has no root of a0 modulo 37, so the denominator D creates no actual
gate there. Prime 557 needs a separate direct row.

1.3 Exact exceptional-prime classification.

  disc(a0) = -178513008142644
           = -2^2*3^3*7^2*29^2*107*557*673,

  |Res_m(a0(m),2m+5)| = 241144 = 2^3*43*701.

Therefore cubic roots are simple for p>=5 outside
{7,29,107,557,673}, and midpoint/cubic roots are disjoint outside {43,701}.
A generic prime has at most four distinct singular residues: the midpoint and
at most three cubic roots. At p=43 or 701 a combined certificate must account
for the sum of the two valuations; separate MID and CUB bounds give only their
maximum.

1.4 Ordinary-step / finite-gate lemma (proved recurrence algebra).

Let R be a DVR with residue characteristic p>=5. Fix a digit block ap+r.
Because each c_i is an integer polynomial,

  c_i(ap+r) = c_i(r) (mod p).

For 0<=r<=p-4, if c3(r) is a unit, the recurrence has unit leading
coefficient. It therefore:

  (a) propagates integrality from three consecutive scaled block values to the
      fourth; and
  (b) propagates congruence between two integral block solutions modulo p.

Consequently the only internal residues requiring solution-specific witnesses
are roots of

  (2r+5)a0(r)=0 (mod p).

The factor (r+3)^5 is met only at the digit boundary r=p-3 and is precisely the
allowed five-order digit loss. Thus each block reduces to ordinary propagation
plus at most four gates, represented explicitly by MID and CUB (with the finite
exceptional-prime modifications above). This is the requested
per-(n,p)-witness + small-uniform-identity shape.

1.5 Division-free last-row certificate and implication to (D).

Write n=ap+r, 0<=r<p. Since Q_r is an integer, the witness inequality

  v_p(p^5 P_n-P_a Q_r) >= v_p(P_a)                         (FROB-P_weak)

implies immediately

  v_p(P_n) >= v_p(P_a)-5,                                  (D)

by the ultrametric inequality. When P_a is nonzero, (D) conversely makes
(p^5P_n-P_aQ_r)/P_a p-integral, so FROB-P_weak is the natural saturated,
division-free certificate form of the last row. The stronger clean diagonal
form asks for one additional p:

  p^5P_n = P_a(Q_r+pE_{n,p}), E_{n,p} in Z_p.              (FROB-P_clean)

The exceptional data below show why the weak saturated form, not the clean
diagonal form, must be the theorem used for all primes.

3. HONEST FRONTIER
------------------

No general MID or CUB divisibility certificate for P was proved. Those two
small rows are the surviving arithmetic content. Equivalently, no proof of
FROB-P_weak or (D) was obtained.

The q-row Lucas theorem and recurrence periodicity explain ordinary residues,
but they do not choose the saturated regular lift at a true singular gate. The
Casoratian identifies a singular direction and Phat witnesses it, but the naive
mod-p regular plane collapses at many primes and cannot select P without higher
jets. The full head-window order-five identity, even if proved, would still
need the regular residual, tilded determinant, exceptional-prime saturation,
and general-a transport. None of those has been silently assumed.

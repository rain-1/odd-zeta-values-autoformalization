VERDICT
-------

The adjugate route, restricted as requested to the recurrence and Casoratian,
WALLS.  It does not close the cubic gate, so no complete a=1 descent is claimed.

The exact adjugate recurrence is

  c3(n) adj(Y_(n+1)) = adj(Y_n)
    [[c1,c2,c3],[-c0,0,0],[0,-c0,0]].

At a simple a0-root the saturated adjugate is rank one.  Its row direction is
the CUB left annihilator and its column direction is a relation among the
three solution columns.  The full first jet of Y adj(Y)=det(Y)I passes at all
requested small roots.  But this jet identifies only the regular two-plane;
it does not prove that the named lifted P digit lies in that plane.

This is a structural impossibility for recurrence + Casoratian alone: the
SL3 basis change

  (Q,P,Phat) -> (Q,P+Phat,Phat)

preserves the scalar recurrence and Casoratian while changing named-column
regularity.  The oracle's saturated null direction is in fact mixed at every
simple internal root, not the pure Phat axis.  For example it is `(30,9,30)`
at `(p,r)=(43,19)`.


MISSING INPUT
-------------

For N=p+r and ell_r the CUB row, the missing statement is exactly

  ell_r (p^5 P_N,p^5 P_(N+1),p^5 P_(N+2)) = 0 mod p.

Equivalently one needs the P column of the named one-digit Frobenius connection
matrix.  The recurrence/adjugate supplies ell_r but not that column.  A proof
must evaluate it from the hypergeometric/period determinant (or an equivalent
Dwork theorem).  This is the structural arithmetic input that can distinguish
P from P+Phat and expose Phat's transverse singular coefficient.


UPPER CHAMBER
-------------

The internal upper roots are `(11,6),(13,7),(23,17)`.  At all three offsets
their central-binomial Kummer carry vector is `(1,1,1)`, the extra factorial
block absent from the midpoint proof.  Exact ladders give P-floor `-5`, the
CUB gain, and the observed LEAD identity at each root.  These are finite oracle
gates only; proving their carry-sensitive connection coefficient is precisely
the missing input.

`(11,9)` and `(29,27)` are digit-boundary diagnostics; the latter is also a
multiple, not simple, root modulo 29.  The combined `(43,19)` depth-two gate
still needs a separate theorem, and the lifted `(701,348)` row remains outside
the cache.

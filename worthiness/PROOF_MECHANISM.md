## 1. The elimination-cost lemma (unconditional)

**Lemma 1.** Let V be a 2-dimensional ℚ-vector space with basis e₀, e₂ and
let L ⊂ V* be a lattice with basis γ₁, γ₂ whose pairings are

    ⟨γ₁, e₀⟩ = 1,   ⟨γ₁, e₂⟩ = ζ(2),
    ⟨γ₂, e₀⟩ = 0,   ⟨γ₂, e₂⟩ = (2πi)².

If δ = aγ₁ + bγ₂ ∈ L satisfies ⟨δ, e₂⟩ = 0 and δ ≠ 0, then 24 | a, and the
minimal such class is δ₀ = 24γ₁ + γ₂ with ⟨δ₀, e₀⟩ = 24.

*Proof.* ⟨δ, e₂⟩ = aζ(2) + b(2πi)² = 0. Since ζ(2) = π²/6 and
(2πi)² = −4π², we get ζ(2)/(2πi)² = −1/24, hence b = a/24. Integrality of
b forces 24 | a. With a = 24, b = 1: ⟨δ₀, e₀⟩ = 24. ∎

**Corollary 1.1.** If the true integral lattice L′ ⊇ L is finer, say
[L′ : L] has p-part p^k with γ₂/p^k ∈ L′ (the only direction that can help,
since refining γ₁ changes the normalization of the form itself), the
elimination cost drops from 24 to 24/p^k at p.

The content: *any* ζ(2)-free integral linear functional on this period
structure is a multiple of the 24-fold class — the cost is forced by the
single identity ζ(2) = −(2πi)²/24 (equivalently by the Bernoulli number
B₂ = 1/6). No property of the specific integrals I(a) enters.

## 2. Conditional theorem for the BZ family

Fix admissible a and n; write I = I(a·n) = −2P + (−4P̂)ζ(2) + 2Q(ζ(5)+2ζ(3)ζ(2)),
and let M be the rank-3 motive of the family (paper, Section "Geometry"),
with weight-graded pieces ℚ(0), ℚ(−2), ℚ(−5).

**Hypotheses.**
(H1) *De Rham control:* in an integral de Rham basis (e₀, e₂, e₅) of
M_dR, the class of the integrand ω_{a,n} has coefficients whose common
denominator divides D_ν(a, n) (the all-primes-ν sharpened d-product).
This is the de Rham half of BZ's "considerable effort" claim — their
(incl)+(sharp_incl) machinery, run integrally; it is exactly what their
Barnes-decomposition sketch (their Remark on the decomposition) is designed
to prove.
(H2) *Betti normalization:* the simplex class γ₁ = [Δ] lies in the integral
Betti lattice L = image of integral relative homology, and the sub-lattice
L ∩ W₄ is generated by γ₁|_{W₄}-compatible classes with γ₂ normalized as in
Lemma 1 (⟨γ₂, e₂⟩ = (2πi)²), up to a finite index i₂(a) supported at
{2, 3} (see §3–§4 for why the support claim is natural).

**Theorem 2 (conditional on H1, H2).** For every admissible a and n,

    24 · D_ν(a, n) · I′(a·n) ∈ ℤ ζ(5) + ℤ,

and the constant 24 improves to 24/[index] wherever the lattice is finer
(Corollary 1.1).

*Proof sketch, given H1–H2.* Since Ext¹_{MT(ℤ)ℚ}(ℚ(−2), ℚ(0)) = 0
(K₃(ℤ) ⊗ ℚ = 0), M ⊗ ℚ admits a splitting σ: ℚ(−2) → W₄M, so
E := M/σ(ℚ(−2)) is the 2-extension of ℚ(−5) by ℚ(0) with period ζ(5)
(Ext¹_{MT(ℤ)}(ℚ(−5), ℚ(0)) is 1-dimensional, generated by ζ(5): the
paper's own argument). The linear form I′ = Qζ(5) − P is the pairing of
the image of ω against the image of γ₁ in E. Making σ integral costs, by
Lemma 1 applied to the W₄-period structure, a factor dividing 24 on the
γ₁-coefficients (the F∞-invariant part of E kills both the ζ(2) and — with
the σ-shift acting through the (2πi)²ζ(3) entry — the ζ(3)ζ(2) term; the
shift contaminates the e₅-coefficient by (1/24)·(the P̂-column), which is
why den(P) inherits the ζ(3)-side arithmetic: visible in the data as the
d_{2n}-type factors inside D_ν). H1 bounds the de Rham denominators by
D_ν; multiplying the two sources gives the claim. ∎

The *unconditional* mathematical residue in Theorem 2 is Lemma 1; the
conditional weight is carried by H1 (integral Barnes bookkeeping — hard but
mechanical) and H2 (a lattice computation in H⁸ of the M₀,₈ pair — one
finite computation, never done).

## 3. What the audit measured (data → lattice indices)

The conjectured sharp constant is 12 = 24/2 on P (with the natural integral
coefficient being 2P, i.e. cost 24/2² on the raw pairing), and 2 on P̂.
By Corollary 1.1 this *measures*:

  * p = 3: no refinement — the 3-part of the cost is the full Bernoulli 3.
    (Consistent: 3-adic excess ceiling +1 = ord₃(24), attained 14×.)
  * p = 2: refinement of index 2 relative to the naive (γ₁, γ₂)-span in the
    2P-normalization (excess ceiling +2 = ord₂(24/2), attained 28×).

I.e., **the audit is an experimental measurement of the 2- and 3-adic
structure of the integral Betti lattice of H⁸(M̄₀,₈, ∂) restricted to this
family** — to our knowledge the first such measurement by numerical
integer-relation methods.

## 4. Why only 2 and 3 (heuristic, now with literature anchors)

The obstruction to an *integral* splitting of the ζ(2)-extension is
governed by the torsion of K₃(ℤ) = ℤ/48 = 2⁴·3 (Lee–Szczarba, Ann. Math.
104 (1976); Bernoulli/von Staudt–Clausen for B₂). A universal correction
supported exactly at {2, 3} — and at no prime ≥ 5, across 103 cells — is
the fingerprint this predicts. We do not use this in §2.

Literature status (researched 2026-07-16, motivic-periods survey):
* The identity behind Lemma 1 is published in motivic form:
  ζ^𝔪(2) = −(𝕃^𝔪)²/24 (Brown, "Motivic periods and ℙ¹∖{0,1,∞}",
  arXiv:1407.5165, §2.2). Ext¹_{MT(ℤ)}(ℚ(0),ℚ(2)) = 0 is Brown,
  Ann. Math. 175 (2012), §2.5.
* The closest published analogue of our measured phenomenon:
  Schnetz, "Multiple Deligne values: a data mine with empirically tamed
  denominators" (arXiv:1409.7204) — clearing constants for motivic-period
  expansions supported at exactly {2,3}. Empirical there as well; no
  K-theory mechanism proposed.
* The integral-lattice synthesis (Lemma 1 read as a lattice-index
  statement, and any integral Betti/de Rham lattice computation for
  M̄₀,ₙ pairs) appears to be UNWRITTEN in the literature. Toolkit for H2:
  Keel (Trans. AMS 330 (1992)): H*(M̄₀,ₙ, ℤ) is torsion-free, generated by
  boundary divisors; stable-model integral de Rham; Golyshev–Kerr–Sasaki
  "Apéry extensions" (arXiv:2009.14762) computes exact extension classes
  of the same genre in lower weight (normalization denominators 6, 5, 10).
* Caution: any "periods of MT(ℤ) lie in ℚ[1/24,...]" phrasing seen in
  secondary summaries is an artifact, not a theorem. And the specific
  halving 48 → 24 (Quillen–Lichtenbaum at 2) is OUR inference, unsupported
  by any located source; the honest statement is "supported at {2,3},
  bounded by the Bernoulli constant, indices measured by the data".

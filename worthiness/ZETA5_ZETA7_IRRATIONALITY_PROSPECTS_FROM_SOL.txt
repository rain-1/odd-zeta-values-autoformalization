PROSPECTS FOR PROVING THAT ZETA(5) OR ZETA(7) IS IRRATIONAL
================================================================

Notes from Sol for Fable
17 July 2026


1. THE PAIRWISE TARGET IS MUCH MORE ATTAINABLE THAN ZETA(7) ALONE
-----------------------------------------------------------------

The primitive weight-seven form is

    I'_n = (75/4) q_n zeta(7) - 3 s_n zeta(5) - P_n.

This is already the right object for proving

    at least one of zeta(5), zeta(7) is irrational.

There is no need to eliminate zeta(5), and no need first to construct pure
rational approximations to zeta(7).

Suppose that one proves the following two statements.

(A) Denominator clearing:

    D_n I'_n belongs to
       Z zeta(7) + Z zeta(5) + Z,

with

    D_n^(1/n) -> exp(7)

up to fixed or subexponential factors.  A typical expected form would be

    C d_n^7 I'_n belongs to
       Z zeta(7) + Z zeta(5) + Z,

where C is independent of n.

(B) Nonvanishing and sufficient decay:

    0 < |I'_n|

for infinitely many n, and

    limsup |I'_n|^(1/n) = rho

with

    exp(7) rho < 1.

If both zeta(5) and zeta(7) were rational, multiplication by their fixed common
denominator would turn D_n I'_n into a nonzero integer tending to zero.  This
is impossible.  Therefore (A) and (B) imply

    at least one of zeta(5), zeta(7) is irrational.

This is a simpler target than an irrationality measure for zeta(7), because
the mixed zeta(5) term is useful rather than parasitic.


2. THE DECISIVE NUMERICAL THRESHOLD
-----------------------------------

Under a d_n^7 denominator bound, the critical decay threshold is

    rho < exp(-7) = 0.0009118819655...

If the primitive form I'_n has the smallest reciprocal rate suggested by the
new characteristic polynomial,

    rho = 1/lambda_max
        = 1/6329.260515...
        = 0.00015799634...,

then

    exp(7) rho = exp(7)/6329.260515... = 0.1733... < 1.

Equivalently,

    log(lambda_max) = 8.752938686... > 7,

leaving the positive exponential margin

    8.752938686... - 7 = 1.752938686....

In that scenario the pairwise irrationality theorem follows immediately once
the denominator and nonvanishing statements are proved.

On the other hand, if the primitive decay rate is the intermediate root

    rho = 0.093937379...,

then

    exp(7) rho >> 1,

and the symmetric family cannot prove the pairwise theorem under a d_n^7
denominator cost.

Thus the primitive recurrence, or even reliable exact values of I'_3 and I'_4,
is a high-value go/no-go experiment.


3. WHAT THE NEW RECURRENCE DOES AND DOES NOT GIVE
------------------------------------------------

The newly reconstructed order-4, degree-19 operator governs the leading
coefficient q_n and appears to govern the descended ladder

    q_n, s_n, Phat_n, I''_n.

It does not appear to govern the primitive coefficient P_n or the primitive
form I'_n.  Consequently, one cannot currently assign the smallest root

    0.000157996...

to I'_n merely because it occurs in the characteristic polynomial of the
descended recurrence.

This is the central issue.  The full weight-seven primitive ladder needs its
own operator or a direct saddle-point analysis.

The positive full cellular integral I_n decays extremely rapidly, but it is not
a substitute for I'_n in the pairwise argument: it also contains the descended
zeta(2)-multiple and therefore additional periods involving zeta(3).


4. THE TWO DECISIVE MISSING THEOREMS
------------------------------------

The pairwise irrationality result is reduced to two principal tasks.

First, prove a weight-seven denominator theorem of the form

    C d_n^7 I'_n belongs to
       Z zeta(7) + Z zeta(5) + Z,

or a sharper variant with a smaller exponential denominator cost.

Second, determine the primitive asymptotic rate and prove

    limsup |I'_n|^(1/n) < exp(-7).

Nonvanishing is also required.  It may follow from an integral representation,
a sign theorem, or a recurrence/asymptotic argument once the primitive operator
is known.


5. HONEST PROBABILITY ASSESSMENT
--------------------------------

My present estimate is:

    roughly a 10--20 percent chance that this exact totally symmetric family
    proves that zeta(5) or zeta(7) is irrational.

The chance of extracting major new mathematics from the campaign is much
higher, perhaps 60--75 percent.  In particular, the primitive recurrence,
P_3, and the first serious weight-seven denominator data all look realistically
attainable even if the eventual irrationality threshold is missed.

A more detailed breakdown is:

  * completing an all-n certificate for the discovered q_n recurrence:
      approximately 85--95 percent;

  * obtaining the true P_3 and additional primitive data:
      approximately 60--75 percent;

  * finding the primitive recurrence for P_n and I'_n:
      approximately 40--60 percent;

  * proving a sharp d_n^7-type denominator theorem:
      approximately 30--50 percent;

  * discovering that the primitive decay is strong enough to beat exp(-7):
      approximately 10--25 percent on present evidence.

These figures are judgment calls, not statistical probabilities.  They are
intended to identify where the real risk lies.


6. WHY THE ODDS ARE NOT YET HIGHER
----------------------------------

The available primitive values behave approximately as

    I'_0 = 18.91...,
    I'_1 = 0.0645...,
    I'_2 = 0.00116...,

so the first ratios are about

    I'_1/I'_0 = 0.0034...,
    I'_2/I'_1 = 0.018....

These ratios are increasing.  With only two transitions they prove nothing,
but they look more naturally compatible with convergence toward an
intermediate scale such as 0.0939 than with convergence toward 0.000158.

Furthermore, the recurrence containing the tiny root does not annihilate the
known primitive form.  The optimistic assignment of the tiny root to I'_n is
therefore currently a hope, not evidence supplied by the recurrence.


7. WHAT WOULD CHANGE THE ODDS
-----------------------------

The single most informative next datum is exact I'_3, followed if possible by
I'_4.  Better still is the primitive recurrence.

If the resulting primitive root satisfies

    rho < exp(-7),

I would raise the probability of completing the pairwise irrationality proof
substantially, potentially above 50 percent, because the remaining challenge
would be primarily arithmetic denominator control.

If instead the primitive root is near 0.0939, the totally symmetric family is
arithmetically too weak under a d_n^7 cost.  Attention should then move to
asymmetric parameters and Rhin--Viola group gains.

In general, if

    D_n^(1/n) -> exp(kappa),

the true condition is

    kappa < -log(rho).

For rho = 0.093937..., this requires

    kappa < 2.365...,

which would be an enormous improvement over the crude symmetric value
kappa=7 and therefore seems unlikely for the same symmetric family.  A more
favourable primitive root or a different parameter orbit could change the
balance.


8. RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS
-------------------------

1. Finish the creative-telescoping certificate for the discovered q_n
   recurrence.  This secures the descended rank-four structure as a theorem.

2. Compute exact I'_3 by the primal integral, residue decomposition, or the
   primitive holonomic route.  This is the immediate threshold diagnostic.

3. Search directly for the primitive operator annihilating P_n and I'_n rather
   than assuming that the descended operator transfers.

4. Prove or sharply formulate the d_n^7 denominator theorem for the primitive
   form.

5. If the symmetric primitive rate misses exp(-7), optimize asymmetric
   parameter orbits using the full transformation group and compare the
   denominator exponent kappa with the primitive decay exponent.


9. BOTTOM LINE
--------------

The pairwise theorem

    zeta(5) is irrational or zeta(7) is irrational

is exactly the right ambitious target for the mixed form I'_n.  It does not
require eliminating zeta(5), and the numerical gap

    log(lambda_max) - 7 = 1.75294...

shows that the dream asymptotic scenario would cross the threshold cleanly.

However, the newly discovered recurrence belongs to the descended ladder, not
yet to I'_n.  The project is therefore one primitive recurrence -- or a few
exact primitive values -- away from knowing whether this route is genuinely
Diophantine or only structurally beautiful.

I would not yet bet on the irrationality theorem, but I would strongly pursue
the next gate.  It has unusually high information value and should give a
fairly decisive answer about the power of the symmetric zeta(7) family.

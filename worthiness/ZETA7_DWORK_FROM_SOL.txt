ZETA(7): DWORK BAND, BARNES PAYOFF, AND UPDATED BETS
=====================================================

Notes from Sol for Fable, River, and the two proof agents
19 July 2026


EXECUTIVE VERDICT
-----------------

There are two separate logical gates here.

1.  In the a=1 band the most economical theorem is the denominator-free,
    cross-multiplied Dwork congruence

      q_1 p^5 p_{p+r} - p_1 q_{p+r} == 0 (mod p),

    with an analogous weight-3 congruence for ptilde.  The old head-window
    statement is a stronger three-normalized-digit version of the same local
    calculation.  The two digits missing from PHASE2_BAND_V should occur in
    the p and p^2 coefficients of the shifted factorial blocks.  Full
    p-blocks contribute neither coefficient after the Wolstenholme pairing;
    the surviving shifted-block coefficients must cancel between the two
    companion determinants.  This is a concrete calculation, not yet a
    theorem.

2.  A telescoper for the positive real period I_n proves a recurrence for
    I_n.  By itself it proves no recurrence for q_n, s_n, P_n, Phat_n, or
    I'_n.  Coefficient separation needs either a coefficientwise/formal-period
    certificate or an already proved recurrence for enough of the filtration
    pieces.  In particular, an order-4 telescoper for I_n does not, merely by
    its order and decay, pin P_3.  This distinction is the central soundness
    condition for the Barnes payoff.

Finite checks below are proposed evidence and falsification tests only.


T1.  THE a=1 BAND AS AN EXECUTION-READY CALCULATION
---------------------------------------------------

1.1 Exact objects and the correct band

Let p >= 11 initially, and write

      n = p+r,                 ceil(p/2) <= r <= p-1.

This is exactly n/2 < p <= 2n/3.  The exceptional primes 2, 3, 5, 7 should be
handled only after the clean calculation is proved.  In particular, 7 divides
q_1 in the normalization p_1/q_1=29/28.

Use the repo's exact definitions

      R_n(k) = (n!)^4 (k+n/2)
               prod_{m=1}^n (k-m)(k+n+m)
               / prod_{m=0}^n (k+m)^6,

      R_n(k) = sum_{i=1}^6 sum_{j=0}^n a_{i,j}/(k+j)^i,

      atilde_{i,j} = j(n-j)a_{i,j}+(2j-n)a_{i+1,j}-a_{i+2,j},

      u=sum_j a_{5,j},             w=sum_j a_{3,j},
      v=sum_{i,j} a_{i,j} H_j^(i),

and the tilded analogues.  Then

      q=u*wtilde-utilde*w,
      pnum=wtilde*v-w*vtilde,
      ptilde=u*vtilde-utilde*v.

I write pnum for the sequence called p_n, only in this section, to avoid
confusing it with the prime p.

The hypergeometric kernel in the pole index is

      a_{6,j}=(-1)^n(n/2-j) binom(n,j)^6
               binom(n+j,n) binom(2n-j,n)

             =(-1)^n(n/2-j)(n!)^4 (n+j)!(2n-j)!
               / (j!^7 (n-j)!^7).                         (1)

Split every j-sum, with no overlap, as

      sum_{j=0}^{p+r} F(j)
        = sum_{b=0}^{p-1} F(b) + sum_{b=0}^{r} F(p+b).     (2)

The first sum is the residue block; the second is the tail block.  Keep them
separate until after the companion determinant is formed.  Prematurely
reducing each sum is liable to destroy the cancellation.


1.2 A single exact factorial-block routine

For M=Ap+b with 0<=b<p define

      E_m(x) = prod_{h=1}^m (1+xp/h),

      Phi(A,b) = p^A A! (p-1)!^A b!
                 prod_{t=0}^{A-1} E_{p-1}(t) E_b(A).      (3)

Then the identity

      (Ap+b)! = Phi(A,b)                                  (4)

is exact over Q.  It is the required p-block splitting, including all
multiples p,2p,...,Ap.  Implement factorial arguments by

      digits(M) = (M div p, M mod p)

and replace every factorial in (1) by Phi(digits(M)).  This avoids a case-by-
case transcription error.

For reference, the digits needed in the two j-blocks are as follows.

Head j=b, 0<=b<p:

      n       : (1,r)
      j       : (0,b)
      n-j     : digits(p+r-b)
      n+j     : digits(p+r+b)
      2n-j    : digits(2p+2r-b).

Tail j=p+b, 0<=b<=r:

      n       : (1,r)
      j       : (1,b)
      n-j     : (0,r-b)
      n+j     : digits(2p+r+b)
      2n-j    : digits(p+2r-b).                            (5)

Equations (1), (3), and (5) are directly transcribable into Fraction arithmetic.
As the largest A here is 3, there is no unbounded block parameter in the a=1
proof.


1.3 Pochhammer/factorial expansion through weight 5

Write H_m^(t)=sum_{h=1}^m h^(-t), and abbreviate H_t=H_m^(t).  The exact product
E_m(x), truncated modulo p^6, is

 E_m(x) = 1
   + px H_1
   + p^2 x^2 (H_1^2-H_2)/2
   + p^3 x^3 (H_1^3-3H_1H_2+2H_3)/6
   + p^4 x^4 (H_1^4-6H_1^2H_2+3H_2^2
               +8H_1H_3-6H_4)/24
   + p^5 x^5 (H_1^5-10H_1^3H_2+15H_1H_2^2
               +20H_1^2H_3-20H_2H_3-30H_1H_4+24H_5)/120
   (mod p^6),                                             (6)

provided denominators are p-units.  This is just the elementary-symmetric
expansion of prod(1+xp/h), so it can also be checked by multiplying the exact
factors.  Do both in the test code.

Any Pochhammer ratio should be reduced to (4).  Equivalently, for unit c,

      (c+xp)_m/(c)_m = prod_{h=0}^{m-1}(1+xp/(c+h)),       (7)

and (6) applies with the interval harmonic sums

      H_[c,c+m-1]^(t)=sum_{h=0}^{m-1}(c+h)^(-t).

For reciprocal collision-free factors use, through the required precision,

      (d+cp)^(-t)
        = d^(-t) sum_{h=0}^5 (-1)^h binom(t+h-1,h)
                    (cp/d)^h             (mod p^6),       (8)

where p does not divide d.  If d=0, do not use (8): extract (cp)^(-t) exactly
and record its negative valuation.  This collision/noncollision branch is
essential.


1.4 Lower pole layers, explicitly through derivative weight 5

For each j put

      B_j(y)=y^6 R_n(y-j),       D_t(j)=(d/dy)^t log B_j(y)|_{y=0}.

Then

      D_t(j)=(-1)^(t-1)(t-1)! [
          (n/2-j)^(-t)
        + sum_{m=1}^n (-j-m)^(-t)
        + sum_{m=1}^n (n+m-j)^(-t)
        - 6 sum_{0<=m<=n,m!=j} (m-j)^(-t) ].              (9)

The formula is meant as a signed rational formula; it also handles the signs
of the first product without conventions about harmonic numbers.  At the
central zero n/2-j=0, compute B_j directly by truncated polynomial products,
as lemma_cb_explore.py already does; do not divide by a_{6,j}=0.

For a_{6,j} nonzero, let Y_m be the complete exponential Bell polynomial:

      Y_0=1
      Y_1=D_1
      Y_2=D_1^2+D_2
      Y_3=D_1^3+3D_1D_2+D_3
      Y_4=D_1^4+6D_1^2D_2+3D_2^2+4D_1D_3+D_4
      Y_5=D_1^5+10D_1^3D_2+15D_1D_2^2+10D_1^2D_3
          +10D_2D_3+5D_1D_4+D_5.

Then, exactly,

      a_{6-m,j}=a_{6,j} Y_m(D_1,...,D_m)/m!,  0<=m<=5.  (10)

Equations (8)-(10) expose every p-collision and generate all six pole layers.
The only denominators divisible by p are to be extracted before reduction.


1.5 Harmonic tail block through weight 5

For 0<=b<p,

 H_{p+b}^(t)
   = H_{p-1}^(t)+p^(-t)
     + sum_{h=0}^5 (-1)^h binom(t+h-1,h)p^h H_b^(t+h)
     (mod p^6),                                           (11)

again retaining as many h as the final normalized precision requires.  More
efficiently, assign total weight t+h to H_b^(t+h), and keep total weight <=5
when computing the singular part p^5 v modulo p^3.  Exact Fraction evaluation
must remain the oracle for this truncation.

For p>=11 the full-block inputs needed are precisely

      v_p(H_{p-1}^(1)) >= 2,
      v_p(H_{p-1}^(2)) >= 1,
      v_p(H_{p-1}^(3)) >= 2,
      v_p(H_{p-1}^(4)) >= 1,
      v_p(H_{p-1}^(5)) >= 2.                              (12)

Do not treat these five statements as independent black boxes.  Use the
reversal identity, truncated to the working precision,

 H_{p-1}^(t)
   =(-1)^t sum_{h>=0} binom(t+h-1,h)p^h H_{p-1}^(t+h),    (13)

obtained by replacing k with p-k.  For odd t it relates the first nonzero
digit to the next even block.  Those relations are what cancel the p and p^2
coefficients; mere valuation bounds often lose exactly the two digits at issue.


1.6 Exactly which named block congruences are needed

For the clean p>=11 proof, the required list is:

  (W1) Wolstenholme: H_{p-1}^(1)=0 mod p^2.

  (W2) Generalized full-block harmonic congruences (12), plus the reversal
       relations (13) through total weight 5.  In implementation, retaining
       terms modulo p^3 after the p^5 normalization is enough.

  (LJ) The Ljunggren/Jacobsthal-strength block congruence

         binom(Ap,Bp)/binom(A,B) = 1 mod p^3              (14)

       for the small A,B generated by (5).  What is actually used is the
       slightly more general shifted version obtained from (3) and (6).
       Thus (14) need not be invoked beyond p^3, and it should be proved in
       this special range directly from (6), not cited as a magic theorem.

  (J+) For exceptional block parameters, retain the valuation-aware form of
       Jacobsthal: the difference in (14), after clearing binomial
       denominators, has at least the standard p^3 gain, with any extra
       valuation from A B(A-B) recorded rather than divided away.  This is
       needed only to keep an integral statement when p divides a ladder
       denominator.

No p^5 binomial supercongruence is required.  Weight 5 refers to the depth of
the logarithmic/harmonic expansion, not to a demand that every binomial ratio
be 1 modulo p^5.

Primary-source checks used for this terminology and scope:

  W. Zudilin, A third-order Apery-like recursion for zeta(5),
  https://arxiv.org/abs/math/0206178

  E. Delaygue, T. Rivoal, J. Roques, On Dwork's p-adic formal congruences
  theorem and hypergeometric mirror maps,
  https://arxiv.org/abs/1309.5902

  J. Rosen, Multiple harmonic sums and Wolstenholme's theorem,
  https://arxiv.org/abs/1302.0073

The Delaygue-Rivoal-Roques theorem is relevant technology, but it does not
automatically apply to the determinant ratio here.  Its hypotheses must be
matched to the actual block functions; until then, calling DWORK
"known-technology" is a programmatic assessment, not a citation-based proof.


1.7 The determinant calculation and the two missing digits

Compute the two j-blocks of u,w,v and their companions using (1)-(13).  Form
the determinants only after summation.  There are two targets.

Minimal denominator-closing target, with q_1,p_1 kept as exact integers:

      Delta_5(n,p)=q_1 p^5 pnum_n-p_1 q_n,
      v_p(Delta_5) >= v_p(q_1 q_n)+1,                     (15)

and

      Delta_3(n,p)=q_1 p^3 ptilde_n-ptilde_1 q_n,
      v_p(Delta_3) >= v_p(q_1 q_n)+1.                     (16)

For p not dividing q_1q_n these are exactly the one-digit ratio congruences.
For p dividing a denominator or q_n, (15)-(16), after multiplying by the
known integral normalizers, are the statements to keep; never invert q_n
modulo p.

Stronger head-window target:

      S_i^head=sum_{j=0}^r a_{i,j},
      Sing=sum_{i=1}^6 p^(-i)(-1)^(i+1)S_i^head,

      p^5 Sing - (29/28)u - (101/84)p^2 w = 0 mod p^3,    (17)

and the tilded version.  PHASE2_BAND_V reports that the window E_M relations
force only the first normalized digit of (17).  The proposed expansion predicts:

  - the constant coefficient is the E_M/Frobenius-window digit already found;
  - the coefficient of p vanishes after (13) couples H_1/H_2 and H_3/H_4;
  - the coefficient of p^2 vanishes after the p^3 block-binomial correction
    from (6)/(14) is combined with the Bell terms D_1D_2, D_3, and their
    companion analogues.

Those last two vanishings are the "two missing digits."  This is falsifiable:
print the coefficient of p and p^2 before summing, after each j-block, after
reflection b <-> r-b, and after the determinant.  They are not predicted to
vanish termwise or in either block separately.  If a nonzero residual remains
after the full determinant for one clean symbolic residue class r, the proposed
Wolstenholme-block mechanism is false even if finite numerical ratios continue
to agree.


1.8 Exact-arithmetic test protocol

For each p in 11,13,17,19,23,29 and every r from ceil(p/2) to p-1:

  1. Generate a_{i,j} twice: direct truncated products (repo oracle) and
     (1),(3),(6),(8)-(10).  Require exact equality before reduction.

  2. Generate H_{p+b}^(i) directly and by (11), checking the claimed modulus
     after clearing the displayed negative p-powers.

  3. Store every quantity as p^e times a p-integral unit.  A modular inverse is
     permitted only after asserting that its denominator is a p-unit.

  4. Check (15)-(16), then the stronger (17), and report the first nonzero
     normalized coefficient.  Report valuations, not floating p-adic digits.

  5. Re-run with one deliberately omitted term from Y_5 and one omitted
     full-block correction.  At least one test should fail; this is a useful
     sensitivity control.

  6. Treat p=7 separately in the integer-normalized determinant.  A proof that
     silently divides by 28 is invalid.


1.9 Step 3: a small triangular Frobenius matrix

The ratio congruences alone do not yet prove the existence of a Frobenius
matrix.  They specify its diagonal weights and first column.  Use the filtered
three-vector

      X_n=(q_n,p_n,ptilde_n)^T

and solve, for n=ap+r, a valuation-normalized congruence of the form

      X_{ap+r} = U_r
        [ 1       0       0   ] X_a + p^K E_{a,r},
        [ c_5(r) p^-5     0   ]
        [ c_3(r) tau(r) p^-3  ]                         (18)

where U_r is a p-adic unit and the entries are inferred by exact Cramer
determinants, not floating fits.  A change of filtered basis may set c_5,c_3
to zero; tau is the only possible weight-gap mixing between the weight-5 and
weight-3 extensions.  First fit (18) for a=1,2,3 at fixed p, then demand the
same U_r,c_5,c_3,tau for every available a.

Equivalently, avoid U_r entirely and work with projective coordinates

      rho_n=p_n/q_n,          sigma_n=ptilde_n/q_n.

The diagonal prediction is

      p^5 rho_{ap+r}=rho_a+O(p),
      p^3 sigma_{ap+r}=sigma_a+O(p).                      (19)

To upgrade (19) to a matrix theorem one must also prove a congruence for the
q-coordinate or construct the underlying rank-three Frobenius module.  Plain
Lucas congruences for q_n fail in the repo, so a diagonal matrix must not be
asserted from (19) alone.  The decisive matrix test is the cocycle law for two
digits: the matrix extracted from n=abp^2+sp+r must agree, to the claimed
precision, with the product of the r-step and s-step matrices.  Failure of
that cocycle falsifies the proposed Frobenius descent.


T2.  WHAT A BARNES TELESCOPER DOES AND DOES NOT PROVE
----------------------------------------------------

2.1 The coefficient ladders

The filtration identity is

      I_n = I'_n + zeta(2) I''_n,

      I'_n  = (75/4)q_n zeta(7)-3s_n zeta(5)-P_n,
      I''_n = -9q_n zeta(5)+2s_n zeta(3)-Phat_n.          (20)

Let T=sum_{k=0}^r c_k(n)S^k be the proven telescoper returned by the positive
Barnes sum.

Unconditional payoff:

      T I = 0                                             (21)

on the n-range for which the telescoping certificate and all boundary terms
are proved.  This is already a valuable theorem about a positive cellular
period and its asymptotics.

What (21) does not imply is

      Tq=Ts=TP=TPhat=TI'=TI''=0.                          (22)

Indeed, applying T to (20) gives one real period relation.  Separating its
zeta words would require unproved Q-linear independence statements involving
1,zeta(3),zeta(5),zeta(7),zeta(2)zeta(3),zeta(2)zeta(5), and zeta(2).
Numerical PSLQ is not a substitute.


2.2 Three sound routes to coefficientwise annihilation

Route A: formal-period certificate.

If the Barnes telescoping identity is established before evaluation in a
formal period algebra in which the seven word coefficients in (20) are free,
and every certificate boundary vanishes coefficientwise, then coefficient
comparison proves T annihilates each displayed rational ladder.  The report
must show this symbolic lift; the existence of a rational hypergeometric
summand alone does not supply it.

Route B: independent ladder certificates.

Prove Tq=Ts=TPhat=0 independently (for example by diagonal CT and residue
descent).  Then TI''=0 follows algebraically.  If in addition TI=0 and T is the
same operator, (20) gives TI'=0.  Finally, if Tq=Ts=0, applying T to the formula
for I' gives TP=0 without any zeta-independence assumption.  This route is
particularly clean.

Route C: a common Gauss-Manin module.

Construct a finite-dimensional rational difference module containing all
coefficient functionals, prove that T is its scalar cyclic operator, and prove
that each ladder is a horizontal functional in that module.  Then (22) follows
from module theory.  A motivic rank prediction, by itself, is not this proof.

There is also a useful negative consistency test.  The repo presently gives
strong evidence that q,s,Phat,I'' satisfy the known order-4 operator L_d while
P,I' do not.  Consequently, if the Barnes run returns exactly L_d and Route B
has certified I'', then it cannot also certify the full I unless the present
P/I' conclusion is wrong: TI=TI''=0 would force TI'=0 and then TP=0.


2.3 If the Barnes order is four

An order-4 recurrence for I_n alone does not extend the old decaying-solution
argument to P_3 or I'_3.  It has only four initial degrees of freedom for I,
not for each coefficient in (20).

The argument can become sound under either of the following packages.

Package 1 (same operator, coefficientwise): prove T annihilates q,s,P,Phat.
Then a recurrence relation whose coefficient of X_3 is nonzero and whose
other nonzero indices are among 0,1,2 determines X_3 exactly.  If this uses
the relation at n=-1, the CT theorem must itself extend to n=-1, or the
sequence and certificate must have a separately proved meromorphic/polynomial
continuation there.  A recurrence proved only for n>=0 may not be evaluated at
n=-1 merely because its polynomial coefficients make formal sense.

Package 2 (boundary condition at infinity): prove all of the following.

  - the actual sequence belongs to the four-dimensional solution space of T;
  - the recurrence is regular from some N onward;
  - its asymptotic sectors and multiplicities are rigorously known;
  - the imposed decay leaves a one-dimensional stable subspace;
  - the connection coefficient from X_3 to every excluded sector is nonzero;
  - the actual integral has the required sign/nonvanishing and decay bound.

Then X_0,X_1,X_2 plus the boundary condition determine a unique real X_3.
This is an analytic uniqueness theorem, not automatically an exact rational
evaluation.  To certify a rational P_3 one still needs either an exact
connection formula or a proved denominator grid h together with a rigorous
interval of width <h containing exactly one grid point.  This is precisely
where the current snap remains conditional.

Thus even a proven order-4 T for I does not by itself validate the snapped
P_3.  It can validate it if T also supplies a rigorous interval for I_3 and the
denominator theorem supplies the grid, or if coefficientwise annihilation
supplies an exact recurrence for P.


2.4 If the Barnes order exceeds four

Work in Q(n)<S>, with S n=(n+1)S.  If L_min is the minimal annihilator of I,
then any telescoper T factors

      T = A L_min                                         (23)

so L_min is a right factor.  The direction matters.

Procedure:

  1. Remove content and apparent common polynomial factors; record singular
     integers where leading/trailing coefficients vanish.

  2. Factor T on the right over Q(n).  If the characteristic polynomial
     suggests Q(sqrt(3)), factor there too, but descend any claimed rational
     operator back to Q(n).

  3. For every candidate right factor R, compute R I from rigorous exact
     initial data or, better, produce a CT certificate for R directly.  The
     factorization T=AR does not imply RI=0; it only implies A(RI)=0.

  4. If RI vanishes for ord(R) consecutive nonsingular indices and R is proved
     to govern the sequence from there onward, induction proves RI=0 on that
     range.  Numerical zeros or floating integral values do not suffice.

  5. Certify minimality by showing no proper right factor annihilates I.  A
     cyclic-vector/Wronskian rank computation in the formal period module is
     preferable.  Otherwise test every proper right factor with exact initial
     data and handle singular indices separately.

  6. To obtain a primitive operator L', use one of:

       (a) direct CT for the symbolic-residue expression of I';
       (b) a proved operator for I and a proved operator L_d for I'', followed
           by exact Ore-module elimination of I'=I-zeta(2)I'';
       (c) coefficientwise factor selection in the formal period module.

     In route (b), an annihilator is an lclm and can have order up to the sum
     of the two orders.  A smaller guessed factor must again be certified as a
     right factor that actually kills I'.

  7. Compare L' with data only after normalization by Ore units.  Equality of
     characteristic polynomials, leading asymptotic roots, or 70 finite terms
     is evidence, not an operator identity.

Common pitfalls are left/right reversal, losing boundary terms in an infinite
sum, dividing by a coefficient that vanishes at an integer n, using n=-1
outside the proved range, treating a nonminimal telescoper's roots as the
actual sequence's roots, and separating zeta coefficients without a formal
independence argument.


T3.  UPDATED ODDS AND FALSIFIABLE PREDICTIONS
---------------------------------------------

These are judgment calls, conditional on the reported run state, not
frequentist probabilities.

  Barnes run returns some nonzero proven telescoper:             50-70%.

  Barnes output is already minimal order 4:                      25-45%.
  An order 6-10 nonminimal or mixed-filtration output is at least
  as plausible as a clean order 4.

  All-n proof of the known q/descent order-4 operator, using either
  the Barnes consequences with the soundness conditions above or the
  reserved diagonal route:                                      70-85%.

  Agent A proves the clean a=1 Dwork congruence:                 45-65%.

  Full Phase-2 Dwork descent, including exceptional primes:      25-45%.

  Agent B obtains an exact, unconditional I'_3/P_3 by residues:  55-75%.

  A certified primitive operator for I' and P:                   45-65%.

  A sharp C d_n^7 denominator theorem for I':                    35-55%.

  The symmetric primitive form beats exp(-7):                    2-8%.

  This exact symmetric family proves
  "zeta(5) or zeta(7) is irrational":                            3-10%.

  Major new mathematics from the campaign:                      80-90%.

The last two odds have fallen sharply because the later, still conditional,
P_3 snap and the rigorous positive-series checks strongly predict

      I'_3/I'_2 approximately 0.0306

and lock I' approximately to -zeta(2)I'', which points to sector B.  This is
substantial evidence, not a theorem: the snap still depends on the ladder
identification and denominator grid, and Agent B can overturn it.


Concrete predictions

P1. Barnes order.  My modal outcomes are order 4 for a genuinely new scalar
primitive/full-period operator, or order about 8 for a mixed/lclm operator.
An order-4 result equal to the existing descent operator L_d would be
surprising and would force an immediate audit of coefficientwise scope.

P2. Characteristic roots.  Any operator genuinely governing the positive
full I should contain a sector compatible with 1.58e-4.  If its minimal right
factor selected by I has only the 0.0939 decay scale, the current sector-A
interpretation of the positive integral is falsified.

P3. a=1 valuations.  For clean p>=11 and all ceil(p/2)<=r<p, predict

      v_p(p^5 rho_{p+r}-rho_1) >= 1,
      v_p(p^3 sigma_{p+r}-sigma_1) >= 1,

with the stronger head-window normalized residual (17) at least p^3.
The p and p^2 coefficient sums should vanish only after both j-blocks and both
companions are assembled.

P4. First correction.  After division by the guaranteed p, predict that the
first correction is antisymmetric under r -> p-1-r and has a factor
2r+1-p, explaining the reflection-centre boost.  A symmetric nonzero first
correction at generic r would falsify this derivative-Frobenius model.

P5. Exceptional primes.  At p=7 the ratio statement with 29/28 should fail if
naively inverted, while the cross-multiplied integer determinant should still
meet the CB valuation because the q-coordinate supplies compensation.  Failure
of compensation falsifies the claimed uniform Dwork closure.

P6. Two-digit location.  Deleting (13) should generally expose a nonzero p
coefficient; deleting the p^3 block correction in (6)/(14) should generally
expose a nonzero p^2 coefficient.  If neither deletion changes the result, the
claimed source of the two missing digits is wrong.

P7. Primitive datum.  Agent B should first reproduce n=0,1,2 exactly, including
all signs in (20).  For n=3, the current falsifiable prediction is

      I_3  = 5.6299224184893...e-14,
      P_3  = 23478462179525/69984,
      I'_3 approximately 3.56748e-5,

and hence sector B for I'.  Any exact residue result differing from that
rational P_3, after normalization is reconciled, falsifies the snap and should
raise the sector-A odds sharply.

P8. What falsifies Dwork descent decisively.  One clean p>=11 and one r in the
band for which the exact cross-multiplied Delta_5 in (15) misses one digit; a
dependence of the fitted matrix entries on the leading digit a after all
normalizations; failure of the two-digit cocycle; or an uncompensated
exceptional-prime denominator.  A failure of plain Lucas is not a falsifier;
that failure is already observed and is why the ratio formulation is used.


BOTTOM LINE
-----------

The a=1 calculation is now finite in structure: two j-blocks, factorial block
indices A<=3, Bell derivatives through order 5, and three normalized digits.
The strongest near-term theorem is (15), not an abstract appeal to Dwork.

The Barnes run is highly valuable, but its prize must be labeled correctly.  A
telescoper for I is first a theorem about I.  It becomes a theorem about the
primitive ladder only after formal coefficient separation, independent ladder
certificates, or certified Ore-module extraction.  Order four and rapid decay
alone do not cross that logical gap.
